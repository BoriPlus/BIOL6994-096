Results Summary

Se analiz el conjunto de datos tyre.csv con el objetivo de comparar el desempeo de distintas marcas de neumticos en trminos de kilometraje. Primero, se visualiz los datos utilizando la funcin ggplot() para observar la distribucin del rendimiento entre las marcas. Luego, se construyo un modelo ANOVA de una va (aov(Mileage ~ Brands, data = tyre)) para evaluar si existan diferencias significativas en el kilometraje promedio segn la marca. Los resultados del ANOVA mostraron un efecto significativo de la marca sobre el rendimiento (F = 17.94, p < 0.001), lo que indica que no todas las marcas ofrecen el mismo desempeo.

Luego, se verific los supuestos del modelo. La prueba de ShapiroWilk (W = 0.9872, p = 0.7826) confirm que los residuos seguan una distribucin aproximadamente normal, y la prueba de Levene (p = 0.5592) mostr que las varianzas eran homogneas entre grupos. Cumplidos los supuestos, se aplic la prueba de Tukey HSD para identificar qu marcas diferan significativamente entre s. Los resultados revelaron que los neumticos Falken presentaron un kilometraje significativamente mayor en comparacin con Apollo, CEAT y Bridgestone, con valores de p ajustados inferiores a 0.01. En contraste, Bridgestone obtuvo el menor rendimiento promedio.

En resumen, el anlisis demuestra que la marca influye de manera significativa en el desempeo de los neumticos por millaje en Puerto Rico, siendo Falken la de mejor rendimiento y Bridgestone la de menor. Este tipo de anlisis estadstico permite respaldar decisiones basadas en evidencia en evaluaciones de producto y control de calidad.

The tyre.csv dataset was analyzed with the goal of comparing the performance of different tire brands in terms of mileage. First, the data were visualized using the ggplot() function to observe the distribution of performance among brands. Then, a one-way ANOVA model (aov(Mileage ~ Brands, data = tyre)) was constructed to evaluate whether there were significant differences in average mileage across brands. The ANOVA results showed a significant effect of brand on performance (F = 17.94, p < 0.001), indicating that not all brands provide the same level of performance.

Next, the model assumptions were verified. The ShapiroWilk test (W = 0.9872, p = 0.7826) confirmed that the residuals were approximately normally distributed, and the Levenes test (p = 0.5592) showed that variances were homogeneous across groups. Once the assumptions were met, the Tukey HSD test was applied to identify which brands differed significantly from each other. The results revealed that Falken tires had significantly higher mileage compared to Apollo, CEAT, and Bridgestone, with adjusted p-values below 0.01. In contrast, Bridgestone showed the lowest average performance.

In summary, the analysis demonstrates that tire brand significantly influences mileage performance in Puerto Rico, with Falken showing the best results and Bridgestone the lowest. This type of statistical analysis supports evidence-based decision-making in product evaluation and quality control.
